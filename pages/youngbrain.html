Who Needs Vision Therapy?

At Young Brain Vision Therapy and Rehabilitation Centre, we specialize in helping children and individuals overcome visual challenges through customized vision therapy programs. Our aim is to improve the quality of life by addressing various conditions affecting vision and learning.

Conditions We Address

Squint (மாறுகண்)

Vision therapy helps to correct misalignment of the eyes, improving binocular vision and depth perception.

Amblyopia (சோம்பேறிக்கண்)

Also known as 'lazy eye,' vision therapy strengthens the weaker eye and enhances visual coordination.

Autism (ஆட்டிசம்)

We provide visual exercises tailored to improve visual processing and focus, supporting children with autism in their learning and interaction.

ADHD (குறுகியகவனம்)

For children with attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, vision therapy aids in enhancing concentration and visual tracking skills.

Learning Disabilities/Difficulties (கற்றல்குறைபாடு)

Our therapy is designed to address visual processing issues, enabling better comprehension and academic performance.

Special Children (மனநலம்குன்றியோர்)

We provide personalized care to help children with special needs improve their visual and motor skills, enhancing their daily life.

Visual Perceptual Skills and Training

At Young Brain, we focus on improving visual perceptual skills, which are crucial for learning, reading, and daily activities. These skills enable the brain to interpret and process visual information effectively.

Visual Discrimination

Helping children distinguish between similar shapes, letters, or objects, which is essential for reading and writing.

Visual Memory

Training to enhance the ability to remember visual information, such as recalling words, numbers, or pictures.

Visual-Spatial Relationships

Improving the understanding of how objects relate to each other in space, aiding in problem-solving and hand-eye coordination.

Visual Sequential Memory

Developing the skill to remember and replicate sequences of visual information, such as patterns or series of numbers.

Visual Motor Integration

Strengthening the connection between visual input and physical movement, essential for tasks like handwriting and sports.

Figure-Ground Perception

Training to focus on specific details in a busy background, which helps with reading and staying attentive in noisy environments.
