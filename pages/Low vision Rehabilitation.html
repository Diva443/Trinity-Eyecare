Low Vision Assessment

Detailed Case History

Understanding the cause and progression of vision loss (e.g., macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma). Evaluating the patient’s daily challenges, occupational needs, and lifestyle.

Visual Function Test

Measuring visual acuity, contrast sensitivity, and visual field. Assessing near and distance vision for specific tasks like reading, writing, or watching TV.

Trial With Aids

Testing different optical and non-optical aids during the assessment to determine the most effective solutions.

Optical Aids Available at Trinity Optics

Magnifiers

Handheld, stand, and illuminated magnifiers for reading small text or viewing fine details.

Telescopic Lenses

Designed for distance tasks such as watching TV, reading signs, or recognizing faces.

High-Add Spectacle Lenses

Custom-designed lenses with strong magnification for near-vision tasks.

Filters and Tints

Special lenses to enhance contrast and reduce glare for individuals sensitive to light.

Bioptic Telescopes

Mounted on eyeglasses for hands-free use, ideal for tasks requiring both near and distance vision..

Non-Optical Aids Available at Trinity Optics

Electronic Video Magnifiers(CCtV)

High-resolution displays that magnify text and images for reading and writing tasks.

Digital Handheld Magnifiers

Portable devices with adjustable magnification and contrast settings.

Reading stands and Lamps

Adjustable stands and task lighting to improve reading posture and visibility.

Tactile and Auditory Aids

Talking watches, large-print books, and Braille labels for non-visual tasks.

Mobility Aids

White canes and electronic navigation aids to assist in safe movement.
