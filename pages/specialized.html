Pediatric assessment

Paediatric optometry clinic at Trinity Optics specializes in eye care for children, offering services like vision screenings, eye exams, and treatment for eye conditions such as amblyopia (lazy eye), strabismus (crossed eyes), and refractive errors exclusively for myopia management. The clinic uses child-friendly equipment and techniques to ensure a comfortable and engaging experience for young patients.

Ensuring your child undergoes regular vision screenings is crucial for their overall development. The American Academy of Ophthalmology (AAO) recommends the following screening schedule:

Newborn to 12 months:

During routine well-child visits, infants should have their ocular health assessed, including external inspection, pupillary examination, corneal light reflection, and assessment of fixation and following behavior.

Ages 1 to 4 years:

Continue ocular health assessments at each well-child visit. Instrument-based vision screening can be initiated as early as age 1.

Ages 3 to 5 years:

Between 3 and 5 years, a child’s vision and eye alignment should be checked. Visual acuity should be tested as soon as the child is old enough to read an eye chart. Refractive error status and interventions are very crucial at this stage.

Early detection of issues like amblyopia (lazy eye) or strabismus (crossed eyes) is vital, as these conditions can impact visual development and academic performance.

Binocular Vision Assessment and Vision Therapy

"Addressing binocular vision disorders such as convergence insufficiency, accommodative disorders, and intermittent squints is quite important. Extensive examinations are performed at our clinic

Binocular and orthoptic examinations are crucial for assessing the coordination and teamwork of your eye muscles. These assessments help detect potential binocular vision disorders, which may cause symptoms such as eye strain, headaches, tired eyes, intermittent blurry vision, difficulty maintaining focus, or even sleepiness after prolonged visual tasks.

Additionally, stereopsis assessment—the ability to perceive depth and 3D vision—is an integral part of a comprehensive binocular vision evaluation. Impaired stereopsis can affect tasks like reading, sports performance, or any activity requiring precise hand-eye coordination.

At Trinity Optics, we conduct detailed binocular vision assessments to diagnose accommodation and vergence disorders. These conditions, once identified, are treated using a combination of in-office vision therapy and tailored home vision therapy exercises. Our clinic also offers specialized tools to enhance stereopsis, helping patients achieve optimal depth perception and visual efficiency.

Make an appointment today for a thorough binocular vision and stereopsis assessment at Trinity Optics. We take pride in being one of the few clinics equipped with advanced vision therapy supplies. Visit our store to explore our range of home vision therapy tools!
